Our Heritage & Story

The Elephant of the Region — Rising Again

St. Aloysius College Nyapea (STALCONY) was founded in 1938 in Gulu District by the Brothers of the Sacred Heart (American Brothers) and later relocated to Nyapea in Zombo District, West Nile. For decades it was the undisputed academic giant of the region.

"A student who never studied at St. Aloysius College Nyapea was not considered by locals as a secondary student. Studying at Nyapea was a great prestige."

After a period of difficulty, STALCONY is experiencing a powerful revival — driven by the Old Boys Association, government investment, and renewed community pride. Enrollment has grown from just 97 students in 2017 to over 400 in 2023. The elephant is waking up!

Old Boys Association
Alumni Power

STALCONY Old Boys Association

Since 2019, the STALCONY Old Boys Association has been driving a powerful revival of the college — from scholarships to dormitory renovations. Led by President Dr. Amos Nyathirombo, they are restoring the school's legendary status one project at a time.

🎓

Student Bursary Scheme

Over 70 students enrolled on scholarship by 2023. Partial fee coverage for brilliant but needy students from PLE and UCE.

🏠

Dormitory Renovation

4 out of 7 dormitories facelifted by the OBA. Mattresses, uniforms, and clothing provided to students in need.

🏫

New Classroom Blocks (2023)

2 blocks of 5 classrooms each commissioned in October 2023, funded by Government at UGX 308 million.

📈

Enrollment Growth

Student numbers grew from just 97 in 2017 to over 400 in 2023 — a 4x increase driven by revival efforts.
